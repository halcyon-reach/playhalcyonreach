The pitch

An empire couldn't hold this place. You're not here to.

A space MMO you fly one ship at a time. No fleet at your back, no autopilot doing the boring part, no second screen running while you sleep. Just you, the dark between stations, and whatever you were smart enough to bring.

Combat

Win before the first shot.

Fights are quiet, fast, and almost never about reflexes. Your guns track on their own — the choice that matters is upstream: what you flew, how close you let them get, and whether you read their move before they made it.

Watch a thirty-second fight and you can see exactly who's ahead. So can they — which is why half of them never start.

The world

A pocket of space where the map keeps a little back.

Systems that should sit light-years apart sit hours apart by sail. Stay near the lights and your life is safe and small. Push out, and every mistake costs more — and the things you find get stranger the further you go.

Stakes

Lose the ship. Keep the pilot.

The hull is gone for good, and some of what you fitted to it. The pilot isn't — your skill, your record, everything in storage stays. Real enough to mean something, bounded enough to risk. The wreck stays where it fell, and someone always comes looking.

The Reach is lit. The dock is open.
